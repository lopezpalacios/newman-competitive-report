Front 01 · Declared vs. verifiable capacity

Headline megawatts are almost never the installed ones.

Structural finding: in Mexico there is no public registry of capacity by company. Distributed generation under 0.7 MW per site requires no permit from the CNE (formerly CRE) and is reported only in aggregate and anonymously. Therefore no fleet figure is auditable at the company level: all are self-reported. Only three named projects have third-party corroboration.

Front 06 · Exposure to the 2024–2026 reform

Sheinbaum's reform recenters the State — and rewards storage.

The new Ley del Sector Eléctrico (DOF 18 Mar 2025) repealed the 2014 LIE, reserved ≥54% of energy for CFE, merged the CRE into the new CNE and raised the permit-free distributed generation threshold from 0.5 → 0.7 MW. "Abasto aislado" died; it is replaced by "autoconsumo" (≥0.7 MW, simplified permit). Storage is formally recognized and favored (binding target of 8,647 MW of BESS).

Market structure · Porter's five forces

The structure favors a capital-light entrant.

Rivalry is fought over commoditized solar; hardware suppliers have no power; the only real power node is CFE (regulatory); and buyers reward transparency. No force punishes whoever enters through intelligence instead of the balance sheet.

New entrants Medium-High

Low barrier for the software/intelligence layer; high for the balance-sheet PPA. The 2025 reform raises regulatory complexity as a new filter.

Suppliers Medium

Commoditized panel/inverter/battery, many OEMs (Jinko, Sungrow, Huawei, CATL/BYD) → low power. The exception: CFE as grid and offtaker = high power.

Internal rivalry Medium-High

4 funded incumbents + dozens of installers. Commoditized on solar, differentiated on capital and storage. The intelligence quadrant is empty.

Buyers Medium

Large industrials multi-bid. But the 15–25 year PPA creates lock-in. Transparency shifts power to the buyer — exactly Newman's wedge.

Substitutes Medium ↓

Staying with CFE, diesel, or efficiency-only. The reform makes CFE more expensive and demands storage → substitutes weaken over time.

Intensity assessment based on the six research fronts. Power concentrated in CFE (interconnection, tariff, offtake) is the dominant structural factor; it favors behind-the-meter models that dodge it.

Conclusion · The empty space

Newman isn't another installer. It's the intelligence layer.

All four position themselves as "the installer who lowers your bill". Newman's unfair asset is reading CFE's tariff and bill better than anyone, and proving the savings before the customer signs. That takes it out of the price war and into a category of its own: certainty and intelligence.

1 · Ungated calculator

Upload your bill → number instantly, no email. Niko wrote the frame ("used-car salesman") but didn't build it. Newman ships it as the home page, not as a promise.

2 · Be the AI's answer

Energía Real excluded itself from AI bots. Newman allows them all, structures the content for extraction and becomes the cited source in ChatGPT/Claude. A channel abandoned by the leader.

3 · Storage is the moat

The reform rewards BESS and physics requires it: the sun doesn't touch 30–50% of the bill. Newman anchors in DG ≤0.7 MW + BESS — minimal exposure, favored category.

Per-CFE-tariff pages updated monthly from the source — something no competitor has. The SEO moat only Newman can dig.

Everyone is Spanish-only or with broken hreflang. The plants arriving via nearshoring buy in English. EN/ES done well = uncontested B2B traffic.
